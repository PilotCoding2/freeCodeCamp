How do planes navigate?

There's different ways on how airplanes navigate, and it depends on two things: if it's either a visual or instrument flight. Visual flights use references, time and estimates to navigate, while instrumental flights rely on GPS, radiomagnetic instruments (called Very High Frequency Omnidirectional Ranges, or VORs) and many other ways.

What are the parts of a plane?

Planes have different parts, which contribute differently to flight operations. They consist on 5 main groups:

  1. Engine: This the part that generates thrust. It can be either a jet engine or a reciprocal engine, each with it's own particularities.
  2. Fuselage: This part holds and contains all the other parts of the plane. For example, the wings are attached to it.
  3. Wings: Thanks to wings, planes can gain lift and soar the skies. They are classified depending on the amount a plane has, their shape and position.
  4. Tail: The tail holds two of the three main control surfaces, allowing the plane to rotate on its own axis or going up and down.
  5. Landing Gear: The landing gear allows the plane to land safely and holds the weight of the plane when it's stationed on land.

Each part is essential so planes can fly. Actually, losing any of these parts during flight can lead to dangerous situations. Can you imagine a plane without wings, or without a landing gear? Thus, the pilot must thoroughly check each part of the airplane according to his operation handbook before venturing to fly.

How does meteorology affect planes?

Weather is one of the main components related to security in the aviation industry. Bad weather can cause accidents or incidents, due to low visibility, turbulences and unexpected climatic situations that lead to potential risks.
